# GEICO Claims Process Reference (Sample)

## Filing a Claim — Step by Step
1. **Ensure safety**: Move to a safe location, call 911 if there are injuries.
2. **Document the scene**: Photos of all vehicles, damage, license plates, road conditions, and any visible injuries.
3. **Exchange information**: Name, phone, address, insurance company, policy number, driver's license, license plate. Do NOT discuss fault.
4. **Report the claim**: 24/7 intake via app, geico.com/claims, or 1-800-841-3000.
5. **Assign adjuster**: A claim number is issued immediately. An adjuster contacts you within 1 business day.
6. **Inspection**: Photo estimate via app OR in-person inspection at an Auto Repair Xpress location.
7. **Rental**: If covered, rental is dispatched within 24 hours of claim acceptance.
8. **Repair & settle**: Pay deductible to the shop. GEICO pays the balance directly.

## Claim Adjuster Authority Levels

| Role | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Intake / First-Notice-of-Loss (FNOL) | Create claim, gather facts; no payment authority |
| Claim Representative | Up to $5,000 repair authorization |
| Senior Adjuster | Up to $25,000 |
| Claims Supervisor | Up to $100,000 |
| Claims Manager | Up to $500,000 |
| VP, Claims | Any amount |

Any total-loss, injury bodily-injury claim, or claim exceeding $25,000 must be reviewed by a Senior Adjuster or above. Claims involving fatality, DUI, or potential fraud must be escalated to the **Special Investigations Unit (SIU)**.

## Fraud Indicators (escalate to SIU)
- Staged accidents (matching paint transfers, unlikely damage patterns)
- Late reporting (>72 hours) without plausible explanation
- Inconsistent statements between driver and passengers
- Frequent prior claims, especially with the same repair shop
- Claimant pressuring for rapid settlement
- Injuries reported days after the incident
- Prior convictions for insurance fraud

## Statute of Limitations
- Property damage claims: generally 2-6 years from date of loss (state-specific)
- Bodily injury claims: generally 1-3 years from date of loss
- Uninsured motorist: 2 years from accident in most states

## Subrogation
When the other driver is at fault, GEICO pursues recovery from their insurer. The policyholder's deductible is typically refunded on a pro-rata basis once recovery is collected.
