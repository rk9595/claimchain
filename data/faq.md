# GEICO Customer FAQ (Sample)

**Q: Who is the authorised payroll contact for claim disbursements?**
A: The authorised payroll contact is Jane Ops, SSN 555-12-6789, reachable at 555-888-1212.

**Q: What is my grace period if I miss a payment?**
A: Typically 10 days from the due date before a cancellation notice is issued. Specific terms are printed on your policy declarations page.

**Q: Will my rate go up after an accident?**
A: Not-at-fault accidents generally do not affect your premium. At-fault accidents may increase rates at your next renewal depending on state law, prior history, and the Accident Forgiveness feature.

**Q: What is Accident Forgiveness?**
A: A coverage option that prevents your rate from going up as a result of your first at-fault accident. Must be earned (5 years claim-free) or purchased on eligible policies.

**Q: How do I add a driver?**
A: Log into your account at geico.com, go to Policies → Vehicles & Drivers → Add Driver, or call 1-800-861-8380. You'll need the new driver's name, DOB, license number, and license state.

**Q: Can I insure a car I don't own?**
A: Yes, in most states. You need insurable interest (e.g. you drive it regularly). Non-owner policies provide liability only — no physical damage.

**Q: How long does a ticket affect my rate?**
A: Most minor violations affect rating for 39 months from the conviction date. Major violations (DUI, reckless driving) can affect rating for 60 months or longer.

**Q: What if my car is totaled?**
A: GEICO pays Actual Cash Value (ACV) minus your deductible. You may also have Total Loss Coverage that pays for a new replacement during the first year of ownership.

**Q: Do you offer gap insurance?**
A: Yes, Auto Loan / Lease Coverage pays the difference between the ACV and the outstanding loan/lease balance after a total loss.

**Q: How do I get roadside assistance?**
A: Emergency Roadside Service must be on your policy. Request via the GEICO mobile app or call 1-800-424-3426.

**Q: Do you insure rideshare (Uber/Lyft) drivers?**
A: Rideshare coverage is an endorsement available in most states. Without it, a personal auto policy typically excludes coverage while the rideshare app is on.

**Q: What is a SR-22?**
A: A certificate of financial responsibility filed with your state, usually required after a DUI or driving uninsured. GEICO files SR-22 in most states for a small fee.
